R&D Arable Application - BodemProfiel


By Filipe Salles on Oct 30, 2022

Main purpose of this application:

 

Datasets of soil moisture (like Staringreeks and BOFEK)  can be investigated from a geostatistical point of view in relation to spatial soil profile distribution in different predominant soil moisture conditions. This can be used to access spatial soil moisture patterns and confidently evaluate the minimal number of soil moisture sensors for a given area.

After the homogenous soil moisture areas are mapped, these areas can be identified as management zones and managed independently for irrigation based on soil moisture and crop water requirements. For those areas, the fixed soil moisture sensors could be used to periodically monitor soil moisture or for irrigation systems.

How to use this app


IMPORTANT: Here we are using Well-known text (WKT) representation of coordinate reference systems - EPSG:28992 Projected coordinate system for Netherlands.

If you want to use the conventional GPS system, you will need to use the Web App through a .kml file, as explained below.

How to get a .kml file?


1) Go to Google Earth (https://earth.google.com/).
2) Go to "Search".
3) Enter two coordinates (longitude and latitude) of some point on the farm. This coordinate can be found easily using a smartphone.
4) Click "Draw lines or shapes" at the bottom left of Google Earth.
5) After drawing the lines of the farm, click on "Save Project", then "New Project" and choose the name that best suits you. Click "Save".
6) A new box will appear automatically. Give your polygon a name.
7) In your project (which you just named) click on the "three dots" at the top right of the tab and click on "Export as KML file".
8) Ready, now just save on your computer or celluar and then insert in the appropriate place in the tab "Soil Map - Complete" in our App.

By doing this, you will be able to access the following data:

1) Soil data informations, according to BOFEK2020, BOFEK2022 and Staringreeks.
2) Map with the farm's soil profiles.
4) Indication of how many sensors will be needed in the farm farm.
5) Information on water capacity and limits for the given farm.
