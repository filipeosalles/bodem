MODULE 1

- Soil texture characterisation is given: %sand, %silt, %clay and %o.m.
- Moisture content is given as x% of MWHC or 75% of field capacity.
- MWHC and field capacity of the test soil are not given.

1. Enter the percentages sand, silt, clay and organic matter in Module 1 of the App. In the Output box, your soil has now been assigned a soil texture class.
2. The other modules of the App can not be used.
3. Use this Table (Staringreeks) to look up the tabulated pF value at the intersection of the soil texture class (rows) as assigned in Module 1 of the App (description under point 1), and the actual moisture content at which the test soil is brought (columns).

The Jar Test

If you do not have the values of the percentages of soil contents, there is an alternative.

To evaluate soil texture, use a simple jar test to determine the percentages of sand silt, and clay. Once the percentages are calculated, the soil textural triangle can be used to determine the soil type.
